# Safety Tools

*Descent into Avernus* goes to genuinely dark places — soul trading, torture, body horror, the aftermath of a city-scale atrocity. That's the campaign we signed up for. These tools exist so the darkness stays **fun** instead of becoming something someone has to sit through.

Using one of these is never a big deal, never needs justification, and never earns a follow-up question. That's the whole point.

## Lines and Veils

**Lines** are content that doesn't appear in this campaign at all.
**Veils** are content that can exist in the story, but happens off-screen — we cut away.

We'll set these together at session zero, and you can add to them any time, privately, forever.

> [!important] Our actual list isn't on this public site — on purpose
> This wiki is public, and your boundaries are nobody's business but this table's. The agreed lines and veils live in our group chat (pinned) and in my private notes. If you want something added or removed, message me directly — you never have to say it out loud at the table, and you never have to explain it.

## The X-Card

Anyone — including me — can tap the X-card (or just say "X") at any time. We stop, we back up, we edit past it. No explanation, no discussion, no apology. Play resumes.

You can use it for anything: something in the fiction, a real-world thing it accidentally brushed against, or just "I'm not enjoying where this is going." All equally valid.

## Pause / Rewind

- **"Pause"** — stop the scene for a real-world moment. Break, breather, check-in.
- **"Rewind"** — undo the last thing; we play it differently.

## Open Door

You can step out of any session, at any time, for any reason, without explaining. Get water, take a walk, take the night. The door is always open and nobody's keeping score.

## Check-Ins

- **Session zero:** we set lines and veils together.
- **After heavy sessions:** quick informal check-in, no pressure.
- **Anytime:** message me privately. Adjusting lines mid-campaign is normal and expected — you don't have to know your limits in advance.

## Specific Heads-Up for This Adventure

Given where this campaign goes, flag anything here you'd rather line or veil:

- Devils bargaining for souls; coerced or deceptive contracts
- Torture and mutilation
- Body horror and transformation
- Mass death and its aftermath; refugees, grief, displacement
- Slavery and forced labor
- Religious betrayal / crisis of faith
- Warfare and its civilian cost

You don't have to explain *why* for any of these. "That one" is a complete sentence.

## My Commitment

I'd rather run a slightly less intense campaign that everyone actually wants to show up to than a maximally grim one that costs someone their evening. If I misjudge a scene, tell me — you won't be spoiling anything, and I'd genuinely rather know.
